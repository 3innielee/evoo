Test Your Knowledge


TRUE OR FALSE

Pure olive oil means the oil is of the highest quality.

CLICK TO SEE ANSWER

FALSE

Pure olive oil is actually a lower grade than extra virgin olive oil. It is a refined olive oil, which means it was produced by using solvents and high heat to neutralize the tastes of the oil.

TRUE OR FALSE

Bitterness is a good quality to have in olive oil.

CLICK TO SEE ANSWER

TRUE

Bitterness is actually a characteristic of fresh olive oil. The level of bitterness depends on how ripe the olive is and is a good indicator of an extra virgin olive oil’s antioxidant and anti-inflammatory value.

TRUE OR FALSE

The color of olive oil is one of the best ways to tell the quality of it.

CLICK TO SEE ANSWER

FALSE

Color is actually an unreliable factor. Color is determined by the ripeness of the olives at harvest, however some unscrupulous producers have been known to disguise their oil’s golden color by adding chlorophyll to it.

What is Extra Virgin Olive Oil?

Extra virgin olive oil, or EVOO, is the highest quality grade of olive oil, which according to standards established by the International Olive Council, the EU, and other governing bodies, must meet a series of chemical requirements and be able to pass a panel test which demonstrates both that it posesses some detectable level of olive fruitiness and that it is free of taste flaws.

- Tom Mueller, Extra Virginity: The Sublime and Scandalous World of Olive Oil
