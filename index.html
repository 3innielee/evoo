Test Your Knowledge


The UC Davis Olive Center conducted a survey in 2012 with 2,234 US Consumers to examine their perceptions and attitudes toward olive oil. The survey results showed that consumers believed that they knew more about olive oil than they actually do. Try to answer the questions below!


TRUE OR FALSE

Pure olive oil means the oil is of the highest quality.

CLICK TO SEE ANSWER

FALSE

47% of participants responded incorrectly!

Pure olive oil is actually a lower grade than extra virgin olive oil. It is a refined olive oil, which means it was produced by using solvents and high heat to neutralize the tastes of the oil.

TRUE OR FALSE

Bitterness is a good quality to have in olive oil.

CLICK TO SEE ANSWER

TRUE

Bitterness is actually a characteristic of fresh olive oil. The level of bitterness depends on how ripe the olive is and is a good indicator of an extra virgin olive oil’s antioxidant and anti-inflammatory value.

TRUE OR FALSE

The color of olive oil is one of the best ways to tell the quality of it.

CLICK TO SEE ANSWER

FALSE

Color was noted as extremely important or very important by about half of the respondents.

However, color is actually an unreliable factor. Color is determined by the ripeness of the olives at harvest.

What is Extra Virgin Olive Oil?

Extra virgin olive oil, or EVOO, is the highest quality grade of olive oil, which according to standards established by the International Olive Council, the EU, and other governing bodies, must meet a series of chemical requirements and be able to pass a panel test which demonstrates both that it posesses some detectable level of olive fruitiness and that it is free of taste flaws.

- Tom Mueller, Extra Virginity: The Sublime and Scandalous World of Olive Oil

Standards and Testing




Positive Attributes


Fruity

Olives are fruit so a good olive oil needs to have some degree of fruitiness

Bitter

Taste of oil obtained from unripe olives, perceived on the back of the tongue

Pungent

A peppery sensation in the mouth and throat is a sign of fresh olive oil and signifies the presence of antioxidants





Negative Attributes


Fusty-Muddy

Smells or tastes like sweaty socks or swampy vegetation, defect caused by olives gathered in piles and undergone fermentation

Musty

Flavor of oil obtained from fruit in which fungi and yeast have developed

Winey-Vinegary

Flavor of oil reminiscent of wine or vinegar due to fermentation of the olives



Metallic

Metal flavor from prolonged contact with raw iron during processing or oil storage

Rancid

The most common defect, the oily flavor from eating old nuts or stale crackers

Other Defects

Negatives such as matty, brined, cooked, waste water, frozen, etc.
